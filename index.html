PROJECTS




No child should ever die from a preventable cause. Every day, 22,000 do. "Believe in Zero" is the U.S. Fund for UNICEF's rallying cry. We fervently believe in zero and ask all those outraged by the unnecessary deaths of children to join us. Because it is such a crucial issue, this campaign opens our school year in September.




The UNICEF Snowflake is a beautiful centerpiece of an ambitious program to raise millions of dollars for UNICEF's programs providing immunization, education, health care, nutrition, clean water and sanitation to children in developing nations. In December, A shard of the UNICEF Snowflake hung in New York City is given to groups who raise a specified amount of money.




Created, inspired and powered by kids since 1950, Trick-or-Treat for UNICEF has empowered generations to make lasting change in their global community. We will be trick-or-treating within our school and all over the county during the month of October.




In the devastated and broken country of Haiti after the earthquake, UNICEF works to empower the youth to lead their country forward and to improve their lifestyle. In January, we will focus on how much UNICEF has changed the lives of the youth in Haiti.


Tetanus, a disease that causes repeated and painful convulsions with sensitivity to light and touch, takes the lives of 60,000 innocent infants and their mothers every year. Join us in May as we raise awareness for this issue and raise funds to immunize mothers and infants all over the world against this deadly disease.


Just $1 can supply a child with safe drinking water for 40 days. Get involved with the UNICEF Tap Project during World Water Week in March. The UNICEF Tap Project evolved into a successful and innovative campaign that used a mobile app to save lives by providing children with clean water.
